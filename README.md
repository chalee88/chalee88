## 🧭 About Me

I'm a 3rd-year Biomedical Engineering student (3.93/4.00 GPA) who loves working where **engineering meets neuroscience** — decoding brain signals, catching physiological anomalies, and turning research into real hardware. When I'm not debugging an EEG pipeline, you'll find me in design contests, or leading student initiatives.

## 🔬 What I'm Into

<table>
<tr>
<td width="50%" valign="top">

### 🧠 Brain–Computer Interfaces
Non-invasive EEG-based BCIs, with a focus on **motor imagery decoding** — letting users control devices through imagined movement alone.

### 📡 Neural Signal Decoding
Classical methods (CSP, LDA) meet modern deep learning (EEGNet, CNN-LSTM, GCN), with an eye toward decoders that generalize across subjects and sessions.

</td>
<td width="50%" valign="top">

### 💓 Physiological Signal Processing
Time-frequency analysis, spatial & adaptive filtering for ECG, EEG, and PCG — spanning analog hardware (amplifiers, active filters) and software (MNE, MATLAB).

### 🤖 ML for Clinical Signals
CNNs, LSTMs, GCNs & hybrids for arrhythmia detection, seizure classification, and motor imagery — with a close watch on class imbalance and reproducibility.

</td>
</tr>
</table>

**⚕️ Assistive Neurotechnology** — using BCI and embedded systems to build tools that restore or augment human capability, from posture-monitoring cushions to future work in neuroprosthetics and assistive communication.
